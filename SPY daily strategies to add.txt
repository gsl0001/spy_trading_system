SPY daily strategies to add
1) 20/50 daily trend pullback
Entry: SPY is above the 20-day and 50-day moving averages, then pulls back to the 20-day with a bullish daily candle close.

Exit: Take profit into the prior swing high or use a trailing stop under higher lows.

Risk: Stop below the pullback low; keep risk fixed before entry.

Works best for: Bull trends and calm-to-moderate volatility.

2) Daily breakout above range
Entry: SPY closes above a 10–20 day consolidation high on expanding volume.

Exit: First target at 1R to 2R, then trail under daily higher lows.

Risk: Stop back inside the prior range.

Works best for: Strong momentum and post-news continuation.

3) Daily mean reversion to VWAP/20DMA
Entry: SPY stretches far below the 20-day average or daily VWAP zone after a sharp selloff, then prints a reversal candle.

Exit: Scale out at the 5-day/20-day mean; trail the rest.

Risk: Stop below the panic low.

Works best for: Oversold market flushes and range-bound regimes.

4) Bull put spread on daily support
Entry: SPY is above rising daily support, IV is not too cheap, and price holds a prior daily low or moving average.

Exit: Close at 50% to 80% of max profit or on a daily close below support.

Risk: Defined-risk spread only; size small enough that one loss is manageable.

Works best for: Mildly bullish or sideways markets.

5) Bear call spread on daily resistance
Entry: SPY is below falling resistance, near a prior swing high, and daily momentum weakens.

Exit: Close early when spread captures most of the credit.

Risk: Exit if SPY closes above the short strike or breaks resistance strongly.

Works best for: Weak tape, failed rallies, and high-IV environments.

6) Iron condor on compressed daily range
Entry: SPY sits in a tight daily range, implied volatility is elevated, and expected move is larger than realized movement.

Exit: Buy back at 50% to 70% of max credit or when one side is threatened.

Risk: Defined loss; avoid oversized wings and event risk.

Works best for: Sideways markets and weeks with no major catalyst.

7) Daily inside-bar breakout
Entry: A daily inside bar forms after a strong trend day; trade the break of the mother bar high or low.

Exit: Measured move or next daily support/resistance.

Risk: Stop on the opposite side of the mother bar.

Works best for: Trend continuation or volatility expansion after compression.

8) 3-day pullback in uptrend
Entry: In an established uptrend, SPY pulls back for 2–4 sessions without breaking the weekly structure, then prints a bullish reversal day.

Exit: Prior high, then trail under the rising swing lows.

Risk: Stop under the pullback low or weekly support.

Works best for: Strong bull markets and slow corrections.
